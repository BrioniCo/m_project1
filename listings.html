Burke's Grocer

Part of this large building once served as Burke's grocer shop on the island. A video from RTE made in 1972 shows it bustling with life.

Unfortunately it has been allowed to fall into dereliction, despite the fact that it could comfortably house at least three households in its many extensions built over the years.

It is a particular shame that this building lies empty as it inhabitas a focal point, at the top of the hill between the north harbour and CDM's bar.

This building is owned by an absentee landowner, based in the USA.

Cumar road

This beautiful home was bought in 2009. It has sat empty since then, despite the worsening housing emergency and the threatened closure of the island's only National school.

The owners have performed some sporadic renovations to the home but despite not being in a position to finish the renovations in the past 4 years, they have not passed the house along to someone who needs a home.

The house is situated in convenient walking distance to the national school, the main harbour and the local shop.

This building is owned by a local landowner, based on the island of Cape Clear.

South Harbour Farmhouse

This home has panoramic views of the Atlantic ocean and the Fastnet Lighthouse in the distance.

The house and its surrounding land was bought as an inhabited home. It was unfortunately left to sit empty and so has deteriorated over the past few years.

An extension was built by the former owners and a new roof added. It is fully plumbed with a kitchen and two bathrooms. This beautiful home, perfect for a family, is currently being used to store haybales.

This building is owned by an absentee landowner, based in France.

Sylivia's House

This house was once owned by an American woman named Sylvia who was once the island's unofficial DVD rental service provider. She welcomed all in to her extensive DVD library.

It is clear that Sylvia was very proud of her home as it was well maintained until it was bought by the current owner.

It has beautiful finishings that have been allowed to deteriorate rapidly. A broken velux window on the roof was not fixed by the current owner and so there is now extensive water damage inside.

This building is owned by an absentee landowner, based in the USA.
